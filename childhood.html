Childhood Memories

Childhood is the cradle of our dreams, and mine was no different—full of innocence, pranks, and unforgettable moments. Born in the humble village of Namudoda, my early years were shaped by family laughter, sibling quarrels, and the simple joys of rural life.

The Movie Escapade of 2012

One tale that still rings in my ears is from the year 2012, when my cousin Owino and I sneaked out to watch a movie. We returned home weaving fibs thicker than a spider’s web—telling my Father we had gone to Butula, while Owino claimed Namisi to his mother.

When the truth bubbled to the surface, Mum and Dad had us running errands faster than ants at a sugar feast—anything they asked, we did without a word. That little escapade taught me early that honesty is like a seed: bury it, and it still sprouts sooner or later.

Life as the Firstborn

Despite those childhood antics, life at home was filled with warmth. As the firstborn, I sometimes carried the world on my young shoulders, learning responsibility and leadership from an early age. Yet, there was always time to laugh, to play, and to dream beyond the fields of Namudoda, fostering a spirit of curiosity and resilience.
